Data methodology

Where every
number comes
from.

Every figure in Premia is grounded in a verifiable source or derived from a confirmed transaction. Audience data comes from Nielsen Sports published market reports. CPM rates are calibrated against publicly documented deal values. Asset parameters follow the QI methodology used across professional sport.

The QI methodology, expressed as seconds visible × audience × Quality Index × CPM per second, the recognised global standard for broadcast sponsorship valuation. Premia applies this framework specifically to EuroLeague basketball, a market where this level of analysis has not previously been accessible to clubs.

The valuation formula
Media Value = seconds_visible × (audience ÷ 1,000) × QI × (CPM ÷ 30) × games
The QI (Quality Index) framework is the recognised global standard for broadcast sponsorship valuation. Each asset is scored independently by type. CPM ÷ 30 converts a 30-second broadcast advertising rate to a per-second equivalent. The formula is applied per asset, per game, across a full season.
Step 01
Audience from
Nielsen market data
Nielsen Sports published cumulative 2024-25 EuroLeague TV viewership by country: 459M total viewers, 98% from home markets of clubs.

Per-club audience is derived as: market total × club share ÷ 34 games. Club market share is estimated from relative fan base size within each market.

Notably, Turkey (29.4M) is the fifth-largest EL television market. Greece (96.8M) and Spain (112.4M) are considerably larger, reflecting the dominant role basketball plays in those markets relative to other sports.
Source
Nielsen Sports / EuroLeague press release, April 2025
Step 02
CPM calibrated
from one confirmed deal
The only publicly confirmed EuroLeague basketball jersey deal in our database: Olympiacos BC / Bwin, €2.0M/yr (2018).

Working backwards through the formula gives a Greece market CPM of €6.5 per thousand viewers for a 30-second equivalent. This is plausible for NOVA Sports Greece, a premium pay-TV platform.

Other market CPMs are scaled relative to Greece, adjusted for GDP per capita and pay-TV market maturity. Germany (€8.0), Spain (€7.0), France (€6.0), Turkey (€7.0), Lithuania (€3.0).
Calibration anchor
Olympiacos / Bwin: €2.0M/yr confirmed (public record)
Step 03
Asset parameters
from broadcast analysis
Each sponsorship asset has a defined seconds visible per game and a Quality Index (QI) score based on screen prominence, size, and clarity.

Press backdrops score QI 1.0 (full screen, unobstructed). Jersey front scores QI 0.8 (partial obstruction, movement). LED boards score QI 0.4 (periphery, variable camera angle).

Seconds visible are estimated from broadcast analysis of EuroLeague games. Jersey front: ~800s/game. LED boards: ~360s/game. Press backdrop: ~45s/game (high value per second despite low volume).

What Premia does not claim
Per-club per-game viewership figures are not published publicly. Premia derives these from market-level data in published Nielsen reports, using documented audience distribution methodology. Broadcaster CPM rate cards are confidential commercial arrangements. Premia's CPMs are calibrated against documented deal comparables rather than sourced directly from broadcasters. All deal estimates in the benchmark database that have not been independently verified are clearly labelled ~ Estimated.

Premia does not claim false precision. It applies a rigorous, documented framework to a market that has lacked structured commercial analysis, and distinguishes transparently between verified data and derived estimates throughout.
